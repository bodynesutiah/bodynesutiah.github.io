Radiant vs. Convection Heat: Understanding the Difference#

Deciding between radiant and convection heaters is like choosing between a warm, sunny day and a cozy, crackling fire. Radiant heaters, like the sun’s embrace, warm objects directly, creating localized heat pockets. In contrast, convection heaters, like a roaring fireplace, heat the surrounding air, circulating warm air throughout the room.

Types of space heaters available at Home Depot#

  • Electric space heaters: Electric space heaters are the most common type of space heater. They’re relatively inexpensive to purchase and operate, and they’re easy to use. Just plug them in and turn them on.
  • Gas space heaters: Gas space heaters are more expensive to purchase and operate than electric space heaters, but they can produce more heat. They also require a vent to the outside, so they’re not suitable for all homes.
  • Oil-filled space heaters: Oil-filled space heaters are filled with oil that is heated by an electric element. They take longer to heat up than other types of space heaters, but they also hold heat for a longer period of time.
  • Infrared space heaters: Infrared space heaters use infrared radiation to heat objects directly. They’re very efficient, but they can also be expensive to purchase.

Features and benefits of space heaters#

There are a number of features and benefits to consider when choosing a space heater, including:

  • Size: Space heaters come in a variety of sizes, so you can choose one that’s right for the space you need to heat.
  • Wattage: The wattage of a space heater determines how much heat it can produce. A higher wattage space heater will produce more heat, but it will also consume more energy.
  • Safety features: Some space heaters have safety features such as tip-over switches and overheat protection. These features can help to prevent accidents.
  • Energy efficiency: Some space heaters are more energy-efficient than others. Look for a space heater with an Energy Star rating to save money on your energy bills.
