Small Home Organization Hacks#

Are you tired of living in a cluttered and disorganized home? Do you feel like you’re constantly tripping over toys, clothes, and other belongings? If so, it’s time to take action and implement some small home organization hacks.

With a little effort and creativity, you can turn your small home into a more functional and stylish space. Here are a few tips to get you started:

Declutter and Get Rid of Unnecessary Items#

The first step to organizing your home is to declutter and get rid of unnecessary items. This means being ruthless and getting rid of anything you don’t use or love. You can sell or donate items you no longer need or use, or you can simply throw them away.

Once you’ve decluttered your home, it’s time to start organizing and storing items in small spaces. This can be a challenge, but it’s possible with a little creativity.

  • Use clear containers: Use transparent containers so you can easily see what’s inside them.

Some items, such as clothes, books, and kitchenware, need special storage solutions.

Consider:

  • Using a shoe rack to store shoes
  • Using a bookshelf to store books
  • Using a kitchen cabinet organizer to store kitchenware

Keep Surfaces Clear and Organized#

One of the best ways to keep your small home organized is to keep surfaces clear and organized. This means putting things away as soon as you’re done with them and avoiding letting clutter accumulate.

  • Create a designated spot for keys, mail, and other items you use frequently.
  • Clear off your kitchen counter and table after each meal.
  • Pick up clothes and put them away as soon as you’re done wearing them.

The key to staying organized is to develop good habits. This means:

Create storage solutions for specific items.#

Your home is a reflection of you, so it should be organized in a way that makes you feel comfortable and productive. If you’re struggling to find a place for everything, it’s time to get creative with your storage solutions.

Here are a few tips for creating storage solutions for specific items:

  • Define your storage needs: Before you start shopping for storage containers, take some time to think about what you need to store and where you want to store it. This will help you narrow down your options and choose the right storage solutions for your home.

  • Utilize vertical storage: One of the best ways to maximize space in a small home is to use vertical storage. Hang clothes on racks, use shelves to store books and other items, and install hooks for keys, bags, and coats.

  • Create designated spaces: Assigning a specific place for everything will help you keep your home organized and tidy. For example, you could have a basket for keys by the door, a shelf for shoes in the closet, and a drawer for cleaning supplies under the sink.

  • Use clear containers: Clear containers make it easy to see what’s inside, so you don’t have to dig through a bunch of boxes to find what you’re looking for. This is especially helpful for items that you use frequently, such as spices, snacks, and toiletries.

  • Label your containers: Labeling your storage containers will help you stay organized and find what you’re looking for quickly. You can use stickers, tags, or even just a piece of tape to label your containers.

  • Purge regularly: The best way to keep your home organized is to purge regularly. Get rid of anything you don’t use or need anymore. This will help you free up space and make it easier to find the things you actually need.

Key Takeaways:

  • Evaluate your storage needs and define specific requirements.
  • Maximize vertical space by utilizing shelves, racks, and hooks.
  • Assign designated spaces for items to maintain a tidy environment.
  • Employ clear containers to easily identify stored items.
  • Label containers to save time and effort in finding what you need.
  • Regularly purge unnecessary items to maintain an organized space.

Use Vertical Space Effectively#

Tired of tripping over clutter and feeling cramped in your small home? Maximize your space and live comfortably by utilizing vertical space effectively. Here’s how:

  1. Maximize Walls:

    • Hang shelves, hooks, and pegboards: to store books, décor, kitchen tools, and more.
    • Install floating shelves: above windows, doors, or unused corners for display or storage.

  2. Optimize Closet Space:

    • Use stackable storage bins: or drawers to create vertical storage for clothes, shoes, and accessories.
    • Hang clothes on multiple levels: with double-hangers, cascading hangers, and over-the-door organizers.

  3. Corner Solutions:

    • Install corner shelves: or cabinets to utilize often-neglected corners.
    • Add corner lazy Susans: in kitchen cabinets for easy access to spices and condiments.

  4. Tall Furniture:

    • Opt for tall bookcases, cabinets, and wardrobes: to maximize storage without taking up floor space.
    • Use vertical storage furniture: like armoires and media centers to store various items.

  5. Vertical Gardens:

    • Hang plants from walls or balconies: with macramé hangers or wall-mounted planters.
    • Create vertical gardens: using stacked planters or repurposed items like rain gutters.

  6. Tiered Storage:

    • Use tiered shelves: in pantries, cabinets, and drawers to create more storage levels.
    • Stackable storage containers: can also be used to maximize vertical space.

  7. Baskets and Bins:

    • Use baskets and bins: to organize items on shelves, under beds, or in closets.
    • Label them: to easily find what you’re looking for.

  8. Behind-the-Door Storage:

    • Install over-the-door organizers: for shoes, cleaning supplies, or toiletries.
    • Hang hooks: for keys, jewelry, or scarves.

  9. Floating Desks and Tables:

    • Mount floating desks or tables: to walls to save floor space and create a dedicated workspace.
    • Fold-down tables: can also be used to create extra surfaces when needed.

Key Takeaways:

  • Utilize walls: with shelves, hooks, and floating shelves for storage and display.
  • Optimize closet space: with stackable storage bins, double-hangers, and over-the-door organizers.
  • Make use of corners: with corner shelves, cabinets, and lazy Susans.
  • Opt for tall furniture: like bookcases, cabinets, and armoires to maximize storage.
  • Create vertical gardens: with hanging plants, wall-mounted planters, or stacked planters.
  • Use tiered storage: with shelves, containers, and stackable items.
  • Organize with baskets and bins: on shelves, under beds, or in closets.
  • Maximize behind-the-door space: with over-the-door organizers and hooks.
  • Install floating desks and tables: to save floor space and create dedicated workspaces.

Keep surfaces clear and organized.#

Clutter can quickly take over a small home, making it feel cramped and uncomfortable. To keep your home feeling spacious and tidy, it’s important to keep surfaces clear and organized. Here are a few tips:

  • Clear off kitchen counters: Keep your kitchen counters clear of appliances, dishes, and other clutter. This will make it easier to prepare meals and clean up.
  • Put away clothes: Clean up your clothes after you take them off instead of leaving them on the floor or on furniture.
  • Organize mail and paperwork: Create a system for dealing with mail and paperwork as soon as it comes in. This will help you stay organized and prevent piles of paper from building up.
  • Store items in clear containers: Use clear containers to store items so you can easily see what’s inside. This will make it easier to find what you’re looking for and put things away when you’re done.
  • Use vertical space: Utilize vertical space by hanging things on walls, using shelves, and stacking items. This will help you make the most of your space.
  • Keep surfaces clean: Wipe down surfaces regularly to keep them clean and free of dust and dirt. This will help your home look and feel tidier.

Key Takeaways:

  • Keeping surfaces clear and organized can help make your small home feel more spacious and tidy.
  • Some tips for keeping surfaces clear include decluttering, putting away clothes, organizing mail and paperwork, storing items in clear containers, using vertical space, and wiping down surfaces regularly.
  • Keeping surfaces clear and organized can help you to feel more relaxed and productive in your home.
